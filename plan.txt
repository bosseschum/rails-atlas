Long-Term Vision

The really interesting version of Atlas isn't:

Models

It's:

Controllers
Services
Models
Jobs
Mailers
External APIs

all in one graph.

Imagine opening a Rails app you've never seen and Atlas showing:

CheckoutController
       ↓
CreatePurchase
       ↓
Purchase
       ↓
StripeGateway
       ↓
SendReceiptJob
       ↓
ReceiptMailer

That's the kind of view that can replace hours of code spelunking.

If I were prioritizing the next 10 features:

1. Path highlighting
2. Metrics sidebar
3. Edge labels
4. Association filters
5. Smells panel
6. Controllers
7. Services
8. Jobs
9. Circular dependency detection
10. Architecture diff

Features 1–5 make Atlas feel complete. Features 6–10 are what could make it genuinely stand out from existing Rails ERD and dependency visualization tools.
